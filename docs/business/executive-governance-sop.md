# Executive Governance SOP

Status: Planned. This SOP describes the intended process for CEO, Ju, and Michelle-level governance once activated. No step below is currently automated or live.

## Purpose
Define a repeatable process for how status rolls up through the hierarchy and how strategic decisions are made and approved.

## Trigger
A scheduled reporting cadence occurs, or a department escalates an item requiring executive attention.

## Required Inputs
- Department status and task updates
- Escalated issues from any department
- Human leadership priorities and constraints

## Step-by-Step Process
1. Each department updates its own tasks.md and memory.md
2. Michelle consolidates department status and escalations
3. Michelle resolves what is within her authority and forwards the rest to Ju
4. Ju reviews cross-department items and forwards strategic or high-risk items to the CEO
5. The CEO agent reviews escalations and either decides within its defined authority or forwards to human leadership
6. Decisions are communicated back down through Ju, Michelle, and the relevant department
7. Outcomes are logged in the relevant memory.md files

## Quality Checks
- Every escalation includes context, options considered, and urgency level
- No decision affecting finances, legal standing, or irreversible actions is made without human approval
- Status reporting is accurate and not overstated

## Escalation Rules
- Anything outside a role's defined authority is passed up immediately, not deferred
- Safety-related escalations (for example, from Mental Health) bypass the normal cadence and go straight to a human

## Handoff Rules
- Each level hands off a summary, not raw unfiltered data, unless the receiving level requests detail
- Final decisions are handed back down with clear rationale

## Output Format
Status and escalation summaries following docs/business/daily-operations-report.md and docs/business/weekly-executive-report.md formats.

## Metrics
- Escalation resolution time (target TBD)
- Percentage of decisions requiring human input (tracked, not targeted for reduction)

## Failure Handling
If status information is missing or unreliable, the responsible level flags the gap explicitly rather than presenting incomplete information as a full picture.
