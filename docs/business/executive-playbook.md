# Success Brand Executive Playbook

Status: Planned. This playbook describes the intended executive operating model once the CEO, Ju, and Michelle roles are active. No process below is currently live.

## CEO Role
Sets overall company mission, priorities, and standards. Approves or declines escalated strategic decisions. Answers to human company leadership. Never executes financial, legal, or irreversible actions autonomously.

## Ju Role
Reports directly to the CEO. Oversees Michelle and reviews cross-department escalations before they reach the CEO. Ensures department activity stays aligned with CEO-level priorities.

## Michelle Role
Manages the day-to-day business departments (Sales, Content, Automation, Research, Finance, Workspace, Mental Health, SuccessBrand) and coordinates with Jesse. Resolves department-level issues directly and escalates what she cannot resolve to Ju.

## Department Responsibilities
Each department's mission, scope, and responsibilities are defined in its own agents/<department>/README.md. This playbook governs how those departments interact with executive oversight, not their day-to-day task execution.

## Decision Rights
- Department agents: decide within their documented scope and operating rules
- Michelle: resolves cross-department conflicts within business operations
- Ju: resolves conflicts Michelle cannot, and reviews strategic alignment
- CEO: sets direction and resolves escalations within delegated authority
- Human leadership: final authority on all financial, legal, hiring, and irreversible decisions

## Approval Thresholds
- Placeholder monetary and risk thresholds are to be defined by human leadership before any department is activated
- Until thresholds are defined, all financial, legal, and irreversible decisions require explicit human approval regardless of amount

## Escalation Ladder
Department agent -> Michelle -> Ju -> CEO -> Human leadership, as detailed in docs/business/executive-governance-sop.md and docs/business/agent-handoffs.md. Safety-related escalations (for example, from Mental Health) bypass the ladder and go directly to a human.

## Daily Briefing
Departments update tasks.md and memory.md daily. Michelle reviews for urgent items using docs/business/daily-operations-report.md as the template once active.

## Weekly Review
Michelle consolidates department status into docs/business/weekly-executive-report.md for Ju and CEO review.

## Monthly Review
Ju and Michelle prepare docs/business/monthly-business-review.md for CEO and human leadership review, covering trends, KPIs, and strategic recommendations.

## Risk Management
Risks are logged in the relevant report template as they are identified. Material risks (financial, legal, reputational, or safety-related) are escalated immediately rather than held for the next scheduled review.

## Change Control
No department may move from Planned to Piloting or Active status without sign-off recorded in its config.yaml and confirmation from Michelle, Ju, and a human. Changes to this playbook itself require the same level of sign-off.
