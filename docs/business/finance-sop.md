# Finance Department SOP

Status: Planned. This SOP describes the intended process for the Finance department once activated. No step below is currently automated or live. This department never executes transactions or handles banking credentials.

## Purpose
Define a repeatable process for tracking, reconciling, and reporting on financial data provided by humans.

## Trigger
A new period's financial data is provided, or a scheduled reporting cycle comes due.

## Required Inputs
- Revenue and expense data provided by a human or finance system export
- Closed-deal data from Sales
- Prior period reports for comparison

## Step-by-Step Process
1. Receive financial data from a human-provided source
2. Reconcile figures against prior records and Sales pipeline data
3. Flag any anomalies or unexplained variances
4. Draft a financial summary report
5. Route the draft to Michelle for review
6. Deliver the reviewed report to Ju and CEO leadership
7. Archive the report and update memory.md

## Quality Checks
- All figures traceable to a human-provided source document
- Anomalies are flagged, not silently adjusted
- No projections presented as guaranteed outcomes

## Escalation Rules
- Any anomaly above a materiality threshold (to be defined by leadership): escalate to Michelle immediately
- Any request to execute a transaction: refuse and escalate to a human immediately

## Handoff Rules
- Reports are handed off to Michelle first, then to Ju for executive visibility
- Sales is notified of any pipeline data discrepancies found during reconciliation

## Output Format
Structured financial summary with period, totals, variances, and flagged items clearly labeled as unverified until human sign-off.

## Metrics
- Reporting accuracy (target TBD)
- Reporting timeliness (target TBD)

## Failure Handling
If source data is incomplete or inconsistent, the Finance agent halts reporting on the affected figures, documents the gap, and requests clarification from a human rather than estimating or fabricating numbers.
