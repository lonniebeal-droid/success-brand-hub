# Mental Health Content Safety SOP

Status: Planned. This SOP describes the intended process for the Mental Health department's involvement in reviewing team workload and any wellbeing-related content once activated. No step below is currently automated or live. This department is not a clinical service.

## Purpose
Define a repeatable process for lightweight wellbeing check-ins and for reviewing any content that touches mental health topics before it is published.

## Trigger
A scheduled check-in cycle occurs, a team member reports strain, or Content produces a piece that references mental health, stress, or wellbeing topics.

## Required Inputs
- Shared workload or schedule signals (with human input)
- Draft content referencing wellbeing topics, if applicable
- Existing wellbeing resource list

## Step-by-Step Process
1. Review available workload/schedule signals for the period
2. Note any patterns suggesting overload, with human confirmation
3. If reviewing content, check that it does not give clinical advice, diagnose, or minimize risk
4. Confirm any crisis-related content includes appropriate referrals to professional resources
5. Provide feedback to Content or Michelle
6. Log the check-in or review outcome in tasks.md

## Quality Checks
- No content claims to diagnose, treat, or replace professional care
- Crisis-related content includes a referral to professional or emergency resources
- Language is non-stigmatizing and age-appropriate

## Escalation Rules
- Any signal of serious risk to a person's safety: escalate immediately to a human, bypassing the normal chain
- Ambiguous but concerning content: escalate to Michelle for a second review

## Handoff Rules
- Review notes are handed off to Content for revision or to Michelle for a final decision
- Confirmed safety concerns are handed off directly to a human, not just logged

## Output Format
Review note with a clear approve/revise/escalate decision and reasoning.

## Metrics
- Reviews completed on time (target TBD)
- Escalations requiring human follow-up (tracked, not targeted for reduction)

## Failure Handling
If the agent is uncertain whether content or a situation poses a safety risk, it treats the situation as requiring escalation rather than assuming it is safe.
